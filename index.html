Kelsey Erin Grinde, PhD

 

I am currently a Postdoctoral Teaching Fellow in the Department of Mathematics, Statistics, and Computer Science at Macalester College.

My teaching experience and interests center mostly around introductory statistics, regression, and statistical theory. In all of my courses, I emphasize statistical literacy, real data applications, and modern computing. Statistics is an inherently collaborative, interdisciplinary field, and I love how this is reflected in the makeup of student backgrounds and interests in undergraduate statistics courses.

My research is in the field of statistical genetics, with the end goal of analyzing large genetic datasets to answer questions about human disease and population history. Most recently, my work has focused on developing statistical methods for genetic studies in admixed populations — populations such as African Americans and Hispanics/Latinos with mixed, diverse ancestry. My most recent first-author paper proposes methods for estimating genome-wide significance thresholds that control family-wise error rate in admixture mapping studies: check out my paper in the American Journal of Human Genetics and the corresponding R package on GitHub to learn more.

To get in touch with me, send me an email (kgrinde at macalester dot edu). I am always happy to talk about research, teaching, and (for students) graduate programs and careers in (bio)statistics!
